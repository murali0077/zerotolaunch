08 — Avoid These

The 8 Mistakes That
Kill Zero-to-Launch Attempts

1
Waiting to be "ready"
Perfection is a procrastination strategy. Post your first piece of content today. The imperfect published thing beats the perfect idea in your head every single time.
2
Building in private
If you build your product without telling anyone, you'll launch into silence. Build in public. Every stage of building is content and proof.
3
Starting on 4 platforms
Spreading thin across every platform from Day 1 produces mediocre results everywhere. One platform mastered deeply will always beat four platforms done poorly.
4
Skipping the lead magnet
Social followers are rented. Email subscribers are owned. If you're not converting followers into email subscribers from Week 1, you're building on someone else's land.
5
Pricing too low out of fear
A $7 product signals low value. $47 signals real investment. Price communicates quality — and low prices attract buyers who undervalue your work and overwhelm your support.
6
Launching without proof
Help 3 people for free before your launch. Get their testimonials. Use their exact words on your sales page. No proof = no trust = no sales, regardless of how good the product is.
7
No launch deadline
An open-ended offer is an offer people bookmark and never buy. Every launch needs a deadline: price goes up in 5 days. Without urgency, decisions are deferred indefinitely.
8
Stopping after launch
The launch is the beginning, not the end. Your evergreen funnel, coaching upsell, and next offer turn a one-time event into a recurring revenue engine. Plan Month 4 before Month 3 ends.
